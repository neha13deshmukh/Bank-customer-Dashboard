# Bank-customer-Dashboard

🏦 **Bank Customer Analysis : A Customer Churn & Retention Analytics Project using Power BI**

📌1. **Project Overview:**

The Bank Customer Analysis Dashboard is an interactive Power BI report developed to analyze customer behavior, activity status, credit card adoption, and churn trends within a banking environment.

It focuses on:

Customer retention performance

Monthly exit trends

Active vs inactive customer distribution

Gender-based churn analysis

Credit card ownership insights

2. **Business Problem**:
   
Customer churn is a major concern in the banking industry, directly impacting revenue and long-term profitability.

Banks often struggle with:

Identifying peak churn months

Detecting high-risk customer segments

Monitoring growth in inactive customers

Understanding demographic impact on churn

Measuring retention performance efficiently

Without centralized visualization, interpreting raw customer data becomes time-consuming and less actionable for management teams.

3. **Goal of the Dashboard** 

The objective of this dashboard is to:

Provide a clear overview of total customer performance

Track monthly exit trends and compare with previous months

Analyze segmentation by activity status (Active vs Inactive)

Evaluate demographic influence (Gender-based exits)

Study credit card adoption distribution

Support strategic customer retention initiatives

4. **Tech Stack** 

This project was built using:

Power BI Desktop – Dashboard design and report development

Power Query Editor (PQE) – Data cleaning, transformation, and preprocessing

DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Creation of calculated measures and KPIs

Data Modeling – Establishing table relationships to enable dynamic filtering and aggregation

**File Formats**

.pbix – Development file (includes data, model, and visuals)

.pbit – Template file (structure without data for reuse)

📊 5. **Dashboard Features & Key Visuals**

🔹 1. KPI Summary Cards

Displays high-level performance indicators:

Total Customers: 10,000

Active Customers: 5,151

Inactive Customers: 4,849

Credit Card Holders: 7,055

Non Credit Card Holders: 2,945

Exit Customers: 2,037

Retained Customers: 7,963

➡ Provides an executive-level snapshot of customer health.

🔹 2. Total Customers by Year & Activity Status (Clustered Column Chart)

Compares Active vs Inactive customers from 2016–2019

Shows yearly growth trend

Helps identify whether inactive customers are increasing proportionally

Insight:

While total customers increased yearly, inactive customers also grew significantly — indicating potential future churn risk.

🔹 3. Exit Customers vs Previous Exit Trend (Line Chart)

Monthly comparison of Exit Customers and Previous Exit Customers

November recorded the highest exit count

February had the lowest exit count

Insight:

Seasonal churn patterns can be identified, helping the bank plan targeted retention campaigns during high-risk months.

🔹 4. Exit Customers by Gender (Donut Chart)

Female customers contribute ~56% of total exits

Male customers contribute ~44%

Insight:

Gender-based retention strategies could improve overall customer engagement.

🔹 5. Credit Card Holder Distribution (Pie Chart)

~70% customers hold credit cards

~30% do not

Insight:

Credit card ownership may influence customer engagement and retention levels.

🔹 6. Interactive Filters (Slicers)

Users can filter data by:

Year

Month

Geography Location

Active Category

Gender

➡ Enables detailed segmentation and deeper analysis.
